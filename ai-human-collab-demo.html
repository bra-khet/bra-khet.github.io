USCO 2025 Guidance on Human-AI Works

The U.S. Copyright Office's 2025 AI Policy Report confirms: "Copyright will protect the human-authored elements of a work containing AI-generated material, to the extent those elements are sufficiently creative." Registration is available when the applicant has made sufficient creative choices — selecting which AI outputs to include, arranging them, and adding original expression. Works that are entirely AI-generated without human creative choices remain ineligible for copyright protection.

human_score = selection(20) + transformation(20) + expression(25) + arrangement(15) threshold = 60 pts → protectable human-AI collaborative work

Legal Foundation — USCO 2025 AI Policy

"The Office will register works containing AI-generated material only if a human author contributed sufficient original expression. Sufficient human authorship exists when a human selects or arranges AI-generated content in a sufficiently creative way, or when the human modifies AI-generated content to the degree that the modifications meet the originality standard." U.S. Copyright Office, Copyright and Artificial Intelligence — Part 2: Copyrightability (2025)
"An artist who uses an AI system to generate an image retains copyright in the elements of the final work that reflect the artist's own original expression — for example, in the selection and arrangement of multiple AI-generated images or in human-authored text accompanying the images." Id.

What counts as human creative choice?

Selection — choosing which AI-generated images to include over others reflects editorial judgment, an authorial act recognized since Feist.

Transformation — applying filters, color grading, or cropping changes the expressive content, layering human aesthetic judgment over the raw output.

Expression — adding captions or titles in your own words creates independently authored literary expression.

Arrangement — the structure and layout of a collection is itself copyrightable under 17 U.S.C. § 103(a).
