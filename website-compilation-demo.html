Thin Copyright in Curated Websites

Under 17 U.S.C. § 103(b), copyright in compilations extends only to "the material contributed by the author of such work, as distinguished from the preexisting material." The underlying recipe data (pre-1928 = public domain) belongs to everyone. But the copyright owner of this curated website holds thin copyright in: the creative selection and arrangement of recipes, the personal anecdotes as independently authored literary works, and the original interactive wine-pairing functionality.

Legal Foundation — Compilation Copyright

"The copyright in a compilation or derivative work extends only to the material contributed by the author of such work, as distinguished from the preexisting material employed in the work, and does not imply any exclusive right in the preexisting material." 17 U.S.C. § 103(b)
"As a constitutional matter, copyright protects only those constituent elements of a work that possess more than a de minimis quantum of creativity… The selection, coordination, and arrangement of the facts must be made independently by the compiler and must entail a minimal degree of creativity." Feist Publications, Inc. v. Rural Tel. Serv. Co., 499 U.S. 340, 358 (1991)

What the thin copyright covers

Creative curation (+10 pts) — Choosing which of the thousands of available public-domain recipes to include, and organizing them into thematic categories, reflects editorial judgment that Feist protects as a creative selection.

Personal anecdotes (+15 pts each) — These are independently authored literary works. A personal memory or contextual note about a recipe is original expression regardless of the recipe itself being in the public domain.

Wine pairing tool (+25 pts) — The interactive tool embodies the author's original compilation of pairing knowledge expressed as functional software. The underlying food-wine relationships may be facts, but the curated knowledge base and its implementation is protectable.

What the thin copyright does NOT cover

The underlying recipe data — ingredients, techniques, names — remain in the public domain. Anyone may copy and republish those facts. The copyright owner cannot prevent use of the recipes themselves, only unauthorized copying of the curated selection, anecdotes, and tool code.
