## The 7 Gaps

### Gap 1 — Kilmainham Gaol Booking · CRITICAL

**Date:** May 29 (Dublin Day 2)
**Type:** Operations

Kilmainham Gaol tours are guided-only with capped group sizes. They sell out 28+ days in advance under normal conditions. May 29 falls on the Friday of the June Bank Holiday weekend — the highest-demand period of the year. No booking is confirmed in the current itinerary.

**What's at risk:** Arriving on May 29 without a pre-booked slot is near-certain to mean no entry. This is the single highest-consequence unresolved booking in the trip.

**Action:** Book immediately at kilmainhamgaol.ie. This cannot wait.

---

### Gap 2 — May 31 Drive-Day Timing · HIGH

**Date:** May 31 (Kilkenny → Cashel → Blarney → Cork)
**Type:** Logistics

Rock of Cashel needs 1.5–2 hrs on-site. Blarney Castle needs 1.5–2 hrs, plus the Blarney Stone queue (45–90 min on a busy Sunday). Drive time totals ~2.25 hrs. Combined: 7–8 hrs minimum. No time plan exists for this day.

**What's at risk:** One site gets dropped or both are rushed. Possible late arrival in Cork (after dinner service).

**Action:** Research arrival windows at both sites; confirm whether Blarney takes advance bookings; create a realistic time plan for the day.

---

### Gap 3 — Cork Sunday Programming · HIGH

**Date:** May 31 evening / June 1 morning
**Type:** Operations

The English Market — Cork's most-visited cultural experience — is **closed on Sundays**. Ed and Amy arrive Sunday evening and depart Monday. The only free Cork morning is Sunday, when the Market is unavailable. No alternative is currently planned.

**What's at risk:** Cork stay loses its cultural anchor.

**Viable Sunday alternatives** (all documented in the data, none scheduled):
- Crawford Art Gallery (free admission)
- St Fin Barre's Cathedral
- Honan Chapel (UCC campus)
- Nano Nagle Place

**Action:** Check Sunday opening hours for the alternatives above; choose a 2-hour morning circuit from Shandon Bells Guest House.

---

### Gap 4 — Ross Castle Bank Holiday Queue · SIGNIFICANT

**Date:** June 1 (en route Cork → Killarney)
**Type:** Operations / Seasonal

June 1 is the Ireland June Bank Holiday. Ross Castle (OPW site) is guided-tours only, first-come-first-served — no advance booking exists. Wait times on bank holidays can reach 1.5–2 hours. The exterior and lakeside walk are accessible without a tour.

**What's at risk:** Mid-morning arrival on a bank holiday may mean a prohibitive queue, conflicting with the Dingle arrival window and the West Kerry Sheepdogs session.

**Action:** Plan early arrival at Ross Castle (before 10:00 AM if possible). Have the lakeside walk as a backup if the queue is too long.

---

### Gap 5 — West Kerry Sheepdogs Pre-Booking · SIGNIFICANT

**Date:** June 1 (Dingle, Night 1)
**Type:** Operations

West Kerry Sheepdogs is a small, family-run sheepdog demonstration on a working farm in the Dingle Peninsula — one of the highest-rated experiential activities in the entire dataset (experiential score 9.0). It has limited capacity. June 1 is a bank holiday: high demand. Booking status is unconfirmed.

**What's at risk:** If the June 1 slot fills before booking, this experience is lost with no direct equivalent in the area.

**Action:** Find the booking contact for West Kerry Sheepdogs and confirm or secure a June 1 slot this week.

---

### Gap 6 — Inis Mór Ferry Return Timing · SIGNIFICANT

**Date:** June 5 (Inis Mór day trip)
**Type:** Logistics

A day trip to Inis Mór is planned for June 5. That same evening, the Galway Folk Festival has a 7:30 PM concert (Lisa O'Neill at Townhall Theatre — top-ranked evening event in the dataset). Whether both are feasible depends on the last ferry departure time from Kilronan back to Rossaveal. This has not been confirmed.

Ferry logistics: 40-min crossing + 1-hr drive from Rossaveal to Galway. If the last ferry is at 17:00 or later, the 19:30 concert is reachable.

**What's at risk:** If the last ferry time isn't confirmed, there's no way to plan the evening — and the top-ranked Folk Festival event may be inadvertently passed over.

**Action:** Check the Aran Island Ferries schedule for June 5 (Saturday). Confirm last departure from Kilronan and whether return slots require advance booking.

---

### Gap 7 — Giant's Causeway Pre-Booking · SIGNIFICANT

**Date:** June 7 (en route Donegal → Belfast)
**Type:** Operations

Giant's Causeway (National Trust, Northern Ireland) uses a timed-entry ticketing system for both the visitor centre and shuttle bus. Parking requires advance booking in peak season. June 7 is a Sunday in early summer. No booking is confirmed. The shuttle bus is particularly important for Ed — without it, the return walk from the stones is a significant uphill climb.

The same day includes a Belfast Black Taxi Tour in the evening, creating a firm time constraint on the Causeway visit.

**What's at risk:** No visitor centre access, no shuttle bus, and possibly no parking. The causeway stones are still accessible via coastal path, but the interpretive experience is lost.

**Action:** Book at nationaltrust.org.uk — visitor centre timed entry + shuttle bus for June 7. Confirm car park booking. Look into accessible drop-off alternatives.
