About This Report

Hi Ed and Amy — this document is a gap analysis of your Ireland trip itinerary. A gap analysis is simply a careful look at the plan to find anything that hasn't been confirmed, booked, or thought through — things that could cause a problem on the day if left unaddressed.

Seven such items were found across your May 29 – June 8 itinerary. They range from a critical booking that should be done today, to a handful of things that need a quick phone call or website check in the coming weeks.

Nothing here is cause for alarm — these are normal planning steps for any trip of this scope. This report tells you exactly what to look into and why, in plain language.

#1 CRITICAL — Act Today

Kilmainham Gaol — Booking Required

May 29 · Dublin Day 2
Operations

What's at risk: Missing Kilmainham Gaol entirely — the anchor cultural experience of Dublin Day 2, and one of the most important historical sites in all of Ireland.

Kilmainham Gaol is where the leaders of Ireland's 1916 Easter Rising were executed — a deeply moving and historically significant place. Entry is guided-tour only, and tours consistently sell out more than 28 days in advance. May 29 falls on the Friday before the June Bank Holiday weekend, which is one of the highest-demand periods of the year in Dublin. Without a pre-booked ticket, Ed and Amy will almost certainly be turned away at the door.

There is no walk-in option. Whether a same-day standby queue exists (and what arrival time would be needed) has not been confirmed — but this is a secondary plan at best. The primary step is to book now at kilmainhamgaol.ie.

Honan Chapel

Arts & Crafts gem on the UCC campus. Small, peaceful, extraordinary stained glass.

Nano Nagle Place

Heritage centre and peaceful gardens. Founded by Cork's great social reformer.

#4 SIGNIFICANT

Ross Castle — Bank Holiday Queue (June 1)

June 1 · Bank Holiday
Operations / Seasonal

What's at risk: A 1.5–2 hour queue on a bank holiday could block interior access and throw off the Dingle arrival time.

Ross Castle, on the shores of Lough Leane near Killarney, is a beautiful 15th-century tower house with boat tours available. June 1 is the Ireland June Bank Holiday — one of the busiest days at all Kerry tourist sites.

The important detail: Ross Castle is an OPW heritage site with guided interior tours only — and there is no advance booking system. Tours are first-come-first-served. On bank holidays, wait times can reach 1.5–2 hours. The exterior grounds and lakeside walk are accessible without a tour, but interior access (the historically significant part) could be lost entirely if you arrive mid-morning.

The recommended approach is an early arrival — ideally before 10:00 AM if possible. The lakeside walk is genuinely lovely and worth the stop even if the interior tour queue is too long.

#7 SIGNIFICANT

Giant's Causeway — Pre-Booking Required

June 7 · Causeway Coast
Operations

What's at risk: No visitor centre access, no shuttle bus (important for Ed — the walk is steep), and possibly no parking.

Giant's Causeway in Northern Ireland — 40,000 interlocking basalt columns formed by ancient volcanic activity — is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most dramatic natural wonders in the British Isles. The visitor centre and shuttle bus both require advance tickets managed by the National Trust.

June 7 is a Sunday in peak early-summer season. No booking confirmation is documented. The causeway stones themselves are free to access via the coastal path — but without visitor centre tickets, there's no shuttle bus service. The walk from the visitor centre to the stones involves a significant uphill return, which could be a meaningful physical effort for Ed. Parking at the main car park also requires advance booking in peak season.

This day is also tight: a Belfast Black Taxi Tour is scheduled for the same evening, meaning there's a firm endpoint for the Causeway visit. Book through nationaltrust.org.uk.
