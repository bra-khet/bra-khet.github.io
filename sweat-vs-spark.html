Three creativity factors scored

Title (25%) — Giving the collection a unique name is an authorial act: you're not just labeling data, you're framing it.

Threshold specificity (40%) — The further your quality cutoff from the median (5.0), the more specific your editorial judgment. Choosing "top 5%" is more creative than including everything above average.

Selection proportion (35%) — Including 5–35% of records (a genuine editorial selection, not wholesale copying) maximises this sub-score.

Why Sweat of the Brow Fails

Prior to Feist, some courts protected compilations based on the effort of collecting data. The Supreme Court unanimously rejected this: "The 1976 revisions to the Copyright Act leave no doubt that originality, not 'sweat of the brow,' is the touchstone of copyright protection." 499 U.S. at 359. A thousand hours of data-gathering adds zero copyright; one hour of genuine editorial selection can add thin protection.

Legal Foundation — Rejecting "Sweat of the Brow"

"It may seem unfair that much of the fruit of the compiler's labor may be used by others without compensation. As Justice Brennan noted in his dissent in Harper & Row: 'The primary objective of copyright is not to reward the labor of authors, but to promote the Progress of Science and useful Arts.' … This is the essence of copyright, and it is a constitutional requirement." Feist Publications, Inc. v. Rural Tel. Serv. Co., 499 U.S. 340, 349–350 (1991)
"The key to resolving the tension lies in understanding why facts are not copyrightable. The sine qua non of copyright is originality… Facts do not owe their origin to an act of authorship. The discoverer merely finds and records. He may not claim that the facts are 'original' to him." Id. at 347

Practical significance

This demo simulates a database of collected records. No matter how many data points you generate — representing years of labor — they remain unprotected under Feist. Only the creative act of selecting which records to include and organizing them under an original title creates the modicum of originality needed for thin copyright.
